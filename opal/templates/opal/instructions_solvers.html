Rules and format

  1. The hunt is open to any OTIS students and alums through the OTIS-WEB account.
  2. Each hunt will consist of several feeder puzzles, plus one "meta-puzzle" that uses the answers to all the feeder puzzles.
  3. The answer to each puzzle will be an alphanumeric string. The answers are case-insensitive and whitespace-insensitive.
  4. The answer to the final meta-puzzle will be a valid diamond code and serves as the prize for completing the OPAL hunt.
  5. There is no time limit to the hunt. You can work on them whenever you want.
  6. Guess rate limits: TO BE DETERMINED
  7. There are no constraints on team sizes, but the OPAL hunt is meant to be reasonably solvable by individuals. We think you will have the most fun working in teams of 1-4 people, but that's just a suggestion.
